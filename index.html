About Me

Thanks for stopping by! My name is Jugal Patel and I’m a student journalist in Norfolk, Va. This website contains an overview of some of my work over the past 2 years.

As a writer, I’m most interested in environmental subject matter, particularly adaptation to changing climates, and the human / institutional forces behind those efforts to adapt.

The field itself is all encompassing, which is why it appeals to me. I get to speak with all sorts of people. Scientists, engineers, architects, historians, geographers, communicators, public officials, engaged citizens-- just about anyone you could think of. When the functioning of entire communities are at stake, everyone needs to be involved.

My approach is to tell these stories from a human angle, and include details that pique the readers' senses, immersing them within scenes. Included below are some stories I’ve written that cover the regional process of adapting to sea level rise in coastal Virginia, where sea levels are rising faster than almost anywhere else in the United States.

I’m also interested in innovative journalism. To me, stories in the digital age should be accompanied by data, especially as plotlines weave between narration and explanation. In addition to writing and reporting, I enjoy programming in JavaScript, particularly d3.js and jQuery. I'm currently working towards becoming more nimble as a data journalist, so I’m constantly expanding my digital skills in all aspects, from GIS mapping and data analysis, to design and typography.

Feel free to explore, share or ask me about my work below. I've also included a few other media innovation projects that I’ve coordinated between the Computer Science department at my University and the student newspaper, where I am the Digital Editor.

I hope you find some of my work interesting. Don't hesitate to email me at jpate016@odu.edu if you’d like to get in touch!
